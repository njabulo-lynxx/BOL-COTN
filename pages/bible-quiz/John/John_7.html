JOHN 7

Situation question: who said it, to whom, and when: "You ought to leave here and go to Judea, so that your disciples may see the miracles you do"?

  1. Jesus' brothers said it to Jesus when the Jewish Feast of Tabernacles was near (7:2-3).

In what does no one who wants to become a public figure act?

  1. Secret (7:4).

What is right for Jesus' brothers?

  1. Any time (7:6).

How did Jesus go also to the Feast after his brothers had left?

  1. Not publicly, but in secret (7:10).

What was among the crowds?

  1. Widespread whispering about Jesus (7:12).

When did Jesus go up to the temple courts and begin to teach?

  1. Not until halfway through the Feast (7:14).

From whom does Jesus' teaching come?

  1. From him who sent Jesus (7:16).

According to John chapter 7: verse 20, what did the crowd answer?

  1. "You are demon-possessed. Who is trying to kill you?" (7:20).

By what should the crowd stop judging?

  1. Mere appearances (7:24).

According to John chapter 7: verse 25, what did some of the people of Jerusalem begin to ask at that point?

  1. "Isn't this the man they are trying to kill?" (7:25).

Who is true?

  1. He who sent Jesus (7:28).

Who sent temple guards to arrest Jesus?

  1. The chief priests and the Pharisees (7:32).

Who said to one another, "Where does this man intend to go that we cannot find him?"

  1. The Jews (7:35).

Complete, in essence, the verse from which the following unique word comes. "Streams"?

  1. "Whoever believes in me, as the scripture has said, streams of living water will flow from within him" (7:38).

What is the town where David lived?

  1. Bethlehem (7:42).

Who laid a hand on Jesus?

  1. No one (7:44).

Who retorted, "You mean he has deceived you also?"

  1. The Pharisees (7:47).

Who was one of the Pharisee's own number?

  1. Nicodemus (7:50).

According to John chapter 7: verse 53, where did each go?

  1. To his own home (7:53).
