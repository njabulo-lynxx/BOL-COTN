JOHN 19

Who took Jesus and had him flogged?

  1. Pilate (19:1).

According to John chapter 19. verse 3, what did the soldiers do to Jesus?

  1. They went up to him again and again, saying, "Hail, king of the Jews!" and struck him in the face (19:3).

To whom is Pilate bringing Jesus out?

  1. The Jews (19:4).

Situation question: who said it, to whom, and in reply to what: "You take him and crucify him. As for me, I find no basis for a charge against him"?

  1. Pilate said it to the chief priests and their officials when they shouted, "Crucify! Crucify!" (19:6).

When did Jesus give Pilate no answer?

  1. When Pilate asked Jesus, "Where do you come from?" (19:9).

What if power were not given to Pilate from above?

  1. He would have no power over Jesus (19:11).

Where was the judge's seat?

  1. At a place known as the Stone Pavement (19:13).

Who finally handed Jesus over to the chief priests to be crucified?

  1. Pilate (19:16).

What did Pilate have prepared and fastened to the cross?

  1. A notice which read: JESUS OF NAZARETH, THE KING OF THE JEWS (19:19).

Who read this sign?

  1. Many of the Jews (19:20).

What was seamless, woven in one piece from top to bottom?

  1. Jesus' undergarment (19:23).

Who stood near the cross of Jesus?

  1. His mother, his mother's sister, Mary the wife of Clopas, and Mary Magdalene (19:25).

Who took Jesus' mother into his home from that time on?

  1. The disciple whom Jesus loved (19:27).

What did the soldiers soak in a jar of wine vinegar?

  1. A sponge (19:29).

Why was the next day to be a special Sabbath?

  1. It was the day of Preparation (19:31).

Whose legs did the soldiers not break?

  1. Jesus (19:33)

According to John chapter 19. verse 36, what scripture would be fulfilled?

  1. "Not one of his bones will be broken" (19:36)

How was Joseph a disciple of Jesus?

  1. Secretly (19.38)

Where had no one ever been laid?

  1. In a new tomb in the garden at the place where Jesus was crucified (19:41).

Where did Joseph and Nicodemus lay Jesus?

  1. In a new tomb (in which no one had ever been laid) (19:42)
